# Year-End Close Checklist for Construction Companies

## Overview

Year-end close for construction companies is more complex than typical businesses. You're dealing with WIP adjustments, 1099s for dozens of subcontractors, equipment depreciation, and often an external CPA preparing tax returns.

This comprehensive checklist guides you through the entire year-end process, from initial preparation through final closing and tax filing.

## Key Year-End Deadlines

### Federal Tax Deadlines

| Form | Description | Due Date |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **W-2** | Employee wages | January 31 (to employees AND SSA) |
| **1099-NEC** | Nonemployee compensation | January 31 (to recipients AND IRS) |
| **1099-MISC** | Rents, royalties, other | January 31 (to recipients), February 28/March 31 (to IRS) |
| **940** | Annual FUTA | January 31 |
| **941 (Q4)** | Quarterly payroll | January 31 |
| **1065** | Partnership return | March 15 (K-1s to partners by this date) |
| **1120-S** | S-Corp return | March 15 (K-1s to shareholders by this date) |
| **1120** | C-Corp return | April 15 |
| **Schedule C** | Sole proprietor | April 15 (with personal return) |

### Extension Deadlines

| Entity | Original Due | Extended Due |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| Partnerships (1065) | March 15 | September 15 |
| S-Corps (1120-S) | March 15 | September 15 |
| C-Corps (1120) | April 15 | October 15 |
| Individuals (1040) | April 15 | October 15 |

## Construction-Specific Year-End Issues

### WIP Schedule Finalization

**Critical Items:**
1. Meet with ALL project managers before finalizing cost-to-complete
2. Include only APPROVED change orders in contract values
3. Verify costs match QBO job cost reports
4. Calculate over/under billing for each job separately
5. Document reasoning for any significant estimate changes

### Completed Contract Recognition

If using completed contract method (small contractors):
- Identify all jobs substantially complete in December
- Recognize full revenue and cost for those jobs
- Jobs still in progress: defer recognition

### Retention Considerations

- Retention receivable: Asset on balance sheet
- Retention payable: Liability on balance sheet
- Ensure retention accounts are accurate before year-end
- Consider collectibility of old retention

### Equipment Decisions

Before year-end, decide:
- Section 179 expensing elections
- Bonus depreciation elections
- Timing of equipment purchases (this year vs. next)
- Equipment disposals to recognize

## Common Year-End Mistakes

| Mistake | Impact | Prevention |
|---------|--------|------------|
| Missing W-9s | Can't file accurate 1099s | Collect W-9s throughout year |
| Stale WIP estimates | Incorrect revenue recognition | Monthly PM meetings |
| Unreconciled accounts | Incorrect balances | Monthly reconciliations |
| Late CPA package | Extension required | Start preparation in December |
| Missing 1099 deadline | IRS penalties | Calendar reminder, early prep |
| Incorrect cut-off | Wrong year's expenses | Review transaction dates |
| Forgetting accruals | Understated expenses | Use accrual checklist |
| Not closing the books | Prior year changes | Set closing date in QBO |
