# Audit Preparation Guide for Construction Companies

## Overview

Construction companies face multiple types of audits: CPA financial audits, surety (bonding company) audits, bank audits, insurance audits, and government compliance audits. Each requires different documentation and has different focus areas.

This guide prepares you for any audit scenario and helps you maintain "audit-ready" books year-round.

---

## Types of Audits Construction Companies Face

### Audit Type Comparison

| Audit Type | Who Conducts | Frequency | Primary Focus |
|------------|--------------|-----------|---------------|
| **CPA Financial Audit** | External CPA firm | Annual (if required) | Overall financial statements |
| **CPA Review** | External CPA firm | Annual (common) | Limited assurance on financials |
| **CPA Compilation** | External CPA firm | Annual | No assurance, just formatting |
| **Surety/Bonding Audit** | Bonding company or their CPA | Annual + as needed | WIP, financial strength, backlog |
| **Bank Audit** | Bank or their consultant | Annual + covenants | Collateral, financial ratios |
| **Insurance Audit** | Insurance company | Annual | Payroll verification |
| **Workers' Comp Audit** | WC carrier | Annual | Payroll by classification |
| **Government Compliance** | Government agency | As required | Certified payroll, prevailing wage |
| **IRS Audit** | Internal Revenue Service | Random/triggered | Tax return accuracy |

---

## CPA Financial Statement Audits

### Why You Might Need a CPA Audit

- **Bonding requirement**: Sureties often require audited financials above certain bonding limits
- **Bank requirement**: Lenders may require audits for large credit facilities
- **Ownership changes**: Potential buyers want audited financials
- **Government contracts**: Some require audited statements
- **Growth planning**: Audits provide credibility with stakeholders

## Surety/Bonding Company Audits

### What Sureties Look For

Bonding companies are guaranteeing your performance on contracts. They want to know:

1. **Are you profitable?** - Can you sustain operations?
2. **Do you have adequate working capital?** - Can you fund projects?
3. **Are your estimates reliable?** - Do jobs come in at estimated costs?
4. **What's your backlog?** - How much work do you have under contract?
5. **Are you over-billed?** - Concerning cash flow pattern?
6. **Any problem jobs?** - Jobs losing money?

### Key Surety Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Target Range |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| **Working Capital** | Current Assets - Current Liabilities | Positive, growing |
| **Current Ratio** | Current Assets Ã· Current Liabilities | > 1.3 |
| **Debt to Equity** | Total Debt Ã· Total Equity | < 3.0 |
| **Gross Profit Margin** | Gross Profit Ã· Revenue | 15-25% typical |
| **Backlog to Equity** | Backlog Ã· Equity | < 10-15x |
| **Overhead Ratio** | Overhead Ã· Revenue | < 10% |
| **Revenue per Employee** | Revenue Ã· Employees | Industry benchmarks |

### Surety Red Flags to Avoid

| Red Flag | Why It Concerns Sureties | How to Address |
|----------|--------------------------|----------------|
| **Heavy over-billing** | Front-loading, cash problems | Bill accurately per % complete |
| **Large under-billing** | Financing owner's project | Bill promptly for work completed |
| **Negative gross profit jobs** | Losing money | Explain circumstances, show recovery plan |
| **Declining working capital** | Can't fund operations | Build cash reserves |
| **Excessive debt** | Over-leveraged | Pay down debt |
| **Fade (estimates miss)** | Poor estimating | Improve estimating process |
| **Key person issues** | Succession concerns | Document key person roles |
| **Single large job** | Concentration risk | Diversify backlog |

## Bank Audits & Covenant Compliance

### What Banks Review

Banks want to know if their loan is secure. They focus on:

1. **Collateral coverage** - What secures the loan?
2. **Cash flow** - Can you make payments?
3. **Covenant compliance** - Are you meeting loan terms?
4. **Financial trends** - Getting better or worse?

### Common Bank Covenants

| Covenant | Typical Requirement | Calculation |
|----------|--------------------|--------------|
| **Minimum Working Capital** | > $X | Current Assets - Current Liabilities |
| **Current Ratio** | > 1.25 | Current Assets Ã· Current Liabilities |
| **Debt Service Coverage** | > 1.25 | (Net Income + Depreciation + Interest) Ã· Annual Debt Service |
| **Debt to Equity** | < 3.0 | Total Liabilities Ã· Equity |
| **Maximum Debt** | < $X | Total Debt |

## Year-Round Audit Readiness

### Monthly Audit-Ready Practices

| Practice | Why It Helps |
|----------|--------------|
| **Reconcile all accounts monthly** | No year-end scramble |
| **File invoices with source documents** | Easy retrieval |
| **Maintain organized project files** | Contract support ready |
| **Update WIP monthly** | Always have current schedule |
| **Review AR aging weekly** | No surprise collection issues |
| **Keep W-9s current** | 1099 prep is easy |
| **Document unusual transactions** | Auditors will ask anyway |
| **Maintain equipment log** | Fixed asset support ready |

### Documentation Best Practices

**For Every Transaction:**
1. **WHO** - Vendor/customer name
2. **WHAT** - Description of goods/services
3. **WHEN** - Date of transaction
4. **WHY** - Business purpose
5. **WHICH JOB** - Project assignment

## Common Audit Issues & How to Avoid

### CPA Audit Issues

| Common Issue | Prevention |
|--------------|------------|
| **Unrecorded liabilities** | Cut-off procedures, review January bills |
| **Unsupported WIP estimates** | Monthly PM meetings, document basis |
| **Related party transactions** | Disclose all, document at arm's length |
| **Revenue recognition errors** | Understand ASC 606, consistent application |
| **Fixed asset additions** | Keep all invoices, maintain detailed records |

### Surety Audit Issues

| Common Issue | Prevention |
|--------------|------------|
| **Stale WIP estimates** | Monthly updates, PM sign-off |
| **Missing contract documents** | Complete files for each job |
| **Unexplained variances** | Document estimate changes |
| **Over-billing concerns** | Explain billing strategy |
| **Problem job surprises** | Communicate proactively |

### Insurance Audit Issues

| Common Issue | Prevention |
|--------------|------------|
| **Classification errors** | Verify codes at hire |
| **Uninsured subs** | Collect COIs before payment |
| **Unreported payroll** | Reconcile to 941s |
| **Officer payroll exclusion** | Document election properly |
| **Overtime misstatement** | Track overtime separately |

## Audit Response Protocol

### When Auditors Find Issues

1. **Don't argue immediately** - Understand their concern first
2. **Gather additional support** - Maybe you have documentation they haven't seen
3. **Correct actual errors** - If they're right, fix it
4. **Negotiate gray areas** - Some issues have multiple valid treatments
5. **Document the resolution** - Keep record for next year
6. **Implement fixes** - Prevent recurrence

### Management Letter Response

After audits, you may receive a "management letter" with recommendations:

1. **Review each point** - Understand the issue
2. **Assess severity** - Material weakness vs. suggestion
3. **Develop action plan** - How will you address it?
4. **Assign responsibility** - Who owns the fix?
5. **Set timeline** - When will it be fixed?
6. **Document response** - Write formal response letter
7. **Follow through** - Actually implement changes

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Different audits, different focus** - Know what each auditor wants
2. **Year-round preparation** - Don't wait until audit time
3. **Documentation is everything** - If it's not documented, it didn't happen
4. **WIP is critical** - Construction-specific, auditors focus here
5. **Communication matters** - Proactive disclosure beats surprise findings
6. **Learn from audits** - Use findings to improve processes
7. **Maintain relationships** - Good rapport with auditors helps everyone
