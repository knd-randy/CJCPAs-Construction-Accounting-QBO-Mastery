## Column Definitions

### Contract Value
Original contract amount from signed agreement

### Approved COs
Total of all approved change orders to date

### Revised Contract
Contract Value + Approved COs = Total Current Contract

### Est. Cost (Estimated Total Cost)
Current estimate of total costs to complete the job
- Original estimate adjusted for:
  - Change orders
  - Scope changes
  - Cost overruns/savings
  - Updated from Project Manager

### Cost to Date
Actual costs incurred through the reporting date
- Pull from QBO P&L by Class
- Include: Labor, Materials, Subs, Equipment, Other Direct

### % Complete
**Formula: Cost to Date ÷ Estimated Total Cost**

Example:
- Cost to Date: $400,000
- Est. Total Cost: $1,000,000
- % Complete: 40%

### Earned Revenue
**Formula: Revised Contract × % Complete**

Example:
- Revised Contract: $1,200,000
- % Complete: 40%
- Earned Revenue: $480,000

### Billed to Date
Total amount invoiced to customer through reporting date
- Pull from QBO customer balance or invoice history
- Include all progress billings

### Over/(Under) Billing
**Formula: Billed to Date - Earned Revenue**

- **Positive (Over)**: Billed more than earned = Liability
- **Negative (Under)**: Earned more than billed = Asset

## Tips for Using This Template

1. **Update Monthly** - WIP should be prepared at minimum monthly
2. **Get PM Input** - Project managers must provide cost-to-complete estimates
3. **Reconcile to GL** - WIP totals must tie to balance sheet accounts
4. **Document Changes** - Note significant estimate changes
5. **Review for Reasonableness** - Jobs over 100% complete indicate problems
6. **Watch for Profit Fade** - Declining margins need investigation
7. **Consider Backlog** - Include jobs not yet started but contracted
